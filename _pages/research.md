My research applies systems engineering, mathematical optimization, and policy analysis to address emerging challenges in power systems. I focus on long-term grid planning, resource adequacy, reliability, and resilience, with particular attention to the physical and operational constraints that shape system performance. My work examines both upstream and downstream risks across the evolving grid, including critical material supply chains, grid-supporting equipment such as bulk power transformers and power electronics, their deployment and replacement needs, and the rapid growth of large-scale loads such as AI-driven data centers. I also study the policy and market structures that shape system outcomes, including resource adequacy frameworks, capacity market design, and electricity pricing mechanisms, and how they influence future reliability and resilience.

## 1. Critical Materials Supply Chains for Generation Resources

<img src="/images/Supply_chain_material.png" alt="" style="width:80%; display:block; margin: 0 auto 20px auto;">

Energy transition models often overlook the physical limits of critical material supply chains. My research develops an optimization-based framework to examine how material availability, trade, domestic production, and stocks affect the feasibility and pace of U.S. clean energy deployment.

* **Key Focus:** Quantifying how critical materials such as nickel, silicon, and rare earth elements shape the deployment of wind, solar PV, and lithium-ion battery technologies in the United States.
* **Selected Publications:** [Understanding Supply Chain Constraints for the U.S. Clean Energy Transition](https://www.nature.com/articles/s44406-025-00009-1) (*npj Clean Energy*, 2025)

## 2. Supply-Chain-Constrained Capacity Expansion

<img src="/images/SC-GEP.png" alt="" style="width:80%; display:block; margin: 0 auto 20px auto;">

Building on material-focused supply chain analysis, my research examines how upstream constraints shape long-term power system expansion. I develop generation expansion planning models that explicitly incorporate material availability, manufacturing limits, deployment lead times, and field availability, enabling more realistic assessments of investment timing, technology choice, system cost, and reliability.

* **Key Focus:** Integrating upstream supply chain constraints into capacity expansion planning to evaluate their effects on deployment feasibility, investment decisions, and resource adequacy.
* **Selected Publication:** [Generation Expansion Planning With Upstream Supply Chain Constraints on Materials, Manufacturing, and Deployment](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11363418) (*IEEE Transactions on Energy Markets, Policy and Regulation*, 2025)

## 3. Grid-Supporting Equipment and Power System Expansion

<img src="/images/GSE.png" alt="" style="width:80%; display:block; margin: 0 auto 20px auto;">

My research studies how grid-supporting equipment (GSE), including transformers, converters, inverters, PCS, and UPS systems, can become a binding constraint on power system expansion. By linking equipment deployment, replacement dynamics, material requirements, and upstream supply chains, I evaluate how industrial bottlenecks shape the feasible pace of electrification and grid growth.

* **Key Focus:** Modeling GSE availability and critical material constraints in power system planning.
* **Selected Publication:** *Grid-Supporting Equipment Supply Chains Constrain the Feasible Pace of Power System Expansion* (under review)

My research analyzes regional power system resilience through the lens of policy, planning, and market design. I study how storage, trade, institutional constraints, and evolving load patterns shape reliability, flexibility, and long-term system adequacy.

* **Maryland (U.S.):** Policy analysis on storage, grid resilience, and electric system planning for Maryland.
* **China:** Resource adequacy analysis of Northeast China, with emphasis on interprovincial trade and institutional constraints.
* **Selected Publications:** [Resource adequacy under institutional constraints and the low-carbon energy transition in China](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S030142152600042X) (*Energy Policy*, 2026); [Energy Resilience and Efficiency in Maryland](https://mde.maryland.gov/programs/air/ClimateChange/MCCC/Doclib_ERE/EREWG%20Study%20Report%20--%20Energy%20Resilience%20and%20Efficiency%20in%20Maryland.pdf) (*Report*, 2024)


## 5. Grid Infrastructure, Reliability, and Resilience

<img src="/images/ICARUS.png" alt="" style="width:80%; display:block; margin: 0 auto 20px auto;">

My research studies how rapid load growth, including emerging demand such as AI-driven data centers, affects shared grid infrastructure and reshapes regional power systems. I examine how infrastructure bottlenecks, transmission constraints, and changing market signals influence electricity prices, system planning, reliability, and resilience. I also develop open datasets to support infrastructure-informed analysis of regional power systems, particularly in PJM and other fast-growing load centers.

* **Key Focus:** Linking load growth, grid infrastructure, and market outcomes across regional power systems.
* **Selected Publications:** [Electricity Prices under Rapid Load Growth: The Case of Data Centres in Virginia](https://www.oxfordenergy.org/wpcms/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/OEF-147.pdf#page=64.09) (*Oxford Energy Forum*, 2026); [ICARUS PJM Dataset](https://github.com/HopkinsICARUS/ICARUS-PJM-Dataset) (*open dataset*); [ICARUS Data Center Dataset](https://github.com/HopkinsICARUS/ICARUS-DC-Dataset) (*open dataset*)
